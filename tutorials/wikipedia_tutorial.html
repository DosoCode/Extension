Getting Started

  • Visit https://www.wikipedia.org.
  • Select your preferred language (for example, English).
  • You'll be taken to the English Wikipedia homepage with featured articles and search options.

The Wikipedia Homepage

The Wikipedia homepage has a search bar at the top, links to featured articles, and quick links to categories like Current Events and Random Article. On the left, you'll see links to “Main page,” “Contents,” “Current events,” and “About Wikipedia.”

Searching for Articles

To find information, type a word or phrase into the search bar and press Enter. For example, type “Albert Einstein” and you'll go directly to his article. If Wikipedia has multiple matches, it will show you a list to choose from.

Reading Articles

Articles are structured with:

  • Introduction: A quick summary of the topic.
  • Table of Contents: Links to sections of the article.
  • Sections: Information organized by headings.
  • References: Sources at the bottom that verify information.

Understanding References

References are small numbers in brackets like [1]. They link to the source of the information at the bottom of the page. This helps you check where facts come from and whether they're reliable.

Switching Languages

On the left sidebar, you'll see links to the same article in other languages. Click one to switch. For example, the “Cat” article exists in dozens of languages.

Creating an Account

You don't need an account to read Wikipedia, but creating one gives you benefits:

  • Track your edits and contributions.
  • Create a watchlist of articles to follow.
  • Join discussions with other editors.

Editing Articles

Wikipedia is editable by anyone. To edit:

  • Click the “Edit” or “Edit source” button at the top of an article.
  • Type your changes in simple language.
  • Add references to back up your edits whenever possible.
  • Click “Publish changes” when you're done.

Helpful Tips

  • Use the Table of Contents to jump to sections quickly.
  • Check the “Talk” tab of an article to see discussions about the content.
  • Look at the “History” tab to view past edits.
  • Always verify information by checking references.

Extra Stuff

  • Random Article: Click “Random article” in the left menu to explore something new.
  • Categories: Found at the bottom of articles, these group related topics together.
  • Mobile App: Wikipedia also has a free app for iOS and Android for easier reading on the go.
  • Download Pages: You can save articles as PDFs for offline reading.
  • Wikimedia Projects: Explore related sites like Wikimedia Commons (photos) and Wiktionary (dictionary).
