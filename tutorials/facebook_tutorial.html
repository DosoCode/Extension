Getting Started

  • Visit https://www.facebook.com.
  • If you don't already have an account, click “Create new account.”
  • Enter your name, email or phone number, birthday, and a secure password.
  • Confirm your email or phone to finish signing up.

The Facebook Homepage

Once logged in, you'll see:

  • News Feed: Posts from your friends and pages you follow.
  • Menu Bar: Icons for Home, Watch (videos), Marketplace, Groups, and Notifications.
  • Search Bar: At the top to find people, pages, or topics.
  • Profile Picture: Top right, with links to your profile and settings.

Setting Up Your Profile

Click your name or picture at the top to visit your profile. Here you can:

  • Add a profile picture and cover photo.
  • Write a short bio about yourself.
  • Fill in details like your hometown, job, or favorite interests (optional).

Creating Posts

At the top of your News Feed or profile, click “What's on your mind?” You can type text, upload photos or videos, or share links. Choose your audience (Friends, Public, or Only Me) before clicking “Post.”

Adding Friends

Use the search bar to find friends. Click “Add Friend” on their profile. When they accept, you'll be connected and see each other's updates in your News Feed.

Messaging

Facebook has a built-in messenger. Click the speech bubble icon at the top to chat with friends, send photos, or make video calls. You can also install the separate Messenger app on your phone.

Groups and Pages

Groups are communities around common interests (like hobbies, neighborhoods, or support groups). Pages are for businesses, celebrities, or organizations. You can follow or join to see their updates.

Privacy & Security

Click the down arrow in the top right > “Settings & Privacy.” Here you can:

  • Choose who can see your posts (Friends, Public, or Custom).
  • Control who can send you friend requests or messages.
  • Enable two-factor authentication for extra account security.
  • Review your activity log to manage past posts and likes.

Helpful Tips

  • Use the search bar to quickly find friends, groups, or events.
  • “Like” or “Follow” pages to see their updates.
  • Turn on notifications for close friends so you never miss updates.
  • Be cautious about accepting friend requests from people you don't know.

Extra Stuff

  • Facebook Marketplace: Buy and sell items locally.
  • Events: Create or join events like birthdays, meetups, or concerts.
  • Watch: A section for videos, shows, and live streams.
  • Memories: Facebook shows you past posts and events from years ago.
  • Reels: Short videos, similar to TikTok, to watch or create.
